Rails Girls Cairo 28th February - 1st March 2014

Hello world!

Rails Girls is coming to Cairo for the first time! Join the free two-day crash-course to the exciting world of building web applications with Ruby on Rails.

Applications are now closed. Like our page on Facebook, and follow us on Twitter to find out about future events.

Follow @RailsGirlsCairo
Tweet

You learn designing, prototyping and coding with the help from our coaches.

You need your own laptop, curiosity and a sprinkle of imagination!

Want to help? We are looking for more volunteers and sponsors. Email us.

Friday 28th February

10.00 - 11.00

Registration

Register, get your name tag, and meet new people while we set everything up together.
11.00 - 11.15

Welcome Note

While you are having your morning coffee (or tea), we will run you through the schedule and discuss what the day will be like.
11.15 - 12.00

Installation Fest

Bring your laptop, so we can help you install Ruby on Rails if you haven't done so already using the installation guide.
12.00 - 01.00

Prayer Break

We will pause for an hour for the Friday prayer. Snacks will be available during the hour.
01.00 - 01.30

Session / Introduction to web applications

We will briefly go over web applications, how they are made up, and of what components.
01.30 - 03.00

Bentobox - Understanding Web Apps

We will teach you some 'tech talk', using the bentobox exercise.
03.00 - 03.30

Lightning Talk / Open Source

Tarek Shalaby, the Creative Director at The Planet, will discuss open source, its history, how it is revolutionizing the world, and the women contributions in that field.
03.30 - 04.00

Session / Basics of wireframing and sketching

Learn the basics of wireframing and sketching, why you need them, and how they can help you build better web applications.

Saturday 1st March

10.00 - 10.30

Breakfast

Have some breakfast, grab a coffee and have a nice little chat with your coaches and peers.
10.30 - 12.30

Workshop

Jumpstart your first web application.
12.30 - 01.00

Lightning Talk / Discover Git and Github

Emad El-Said, the co-founder and CEO of Pixelware, will give you an introduction to Git and Github.
01.00 - 02.00

Workshop

Continue working and extend your application.
02.00 - 03.00

Lunch

Have lunch, get a break, chat with people.
03:00 - 03:30

Lightning Talk / Open Data

Listen to Tarek Amr, the local ambassador of the Open Knowledge Foundation in Egypt, while he gives you an introduction to Open Data.
03:30 - 04:00

Lightning Talk

by Minoush Abdel-Meguid, the Managing Director and co-founder of Union Capital Partners.
01.00 - 02.00

Workshop

Get your application online: push it to Github, and deploy it to Heroku.

FAQ

How much does the workshop cost? Nothing, it's free! You just need to be excited!

Who is this aimed for? Women of any age with basic knowledge of working with a computer. We would love to have people of all ages taking part. Please bring your laptop.

Can men attend? Yes. However, keep in mind that we are very limited in resources, so girls are given the priority.

I know how to program - How can I help? We’re also looking for people to be coaches. We'll make sure to walk you through the curriculum before the event starts. Email us
