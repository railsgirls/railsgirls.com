Rails Girls Wellington Sat 29th & Sun 30th March 2014

Kia ora!

Rails Girls comes to Wellington! During this free two-day workshop we'll dive into the magical world of Ruby on Rails.

We'll be running another workshop during the last weekend of March. This format will differ slightly from last year. We aim to take a range of skill levels and form smaller working groups based on experience.

All of the material is available for you to learn at home by yourself but Rails Girls offers a casual and friendly environment to learn with others and a good tutor to student ratio to help get past some tricky areas.

Applications are now closed! Sign up for our email list and we'll let you know when we hold another workshop.

Tweet

You learn designing, prototyping and coding with the help from our coaches. We introduce you to Ruby on Rails as one of the open-source web developing frameworks.

You need your own laptop, curiosity and a sprinkle of imagination!

Want to help? Send us an email if you'd like to be involved - email us.

FAQ

How much does the workshop cost? Nothing, it's free! You just need to be excited!

Who is this aimed at? Rails Girls Wellington is open to all those who identify as women or girls with basic knowledge of working with a computer. We’ve had people of all ages taking part.

Can men attend? Yes, but you need to be accompanied by an interested lady. Also, girls are given a priority.

Do you have a code of conduct? Yes - it is here. We expect attendees, coaches, and sponsor representatives to behave respectfully to make sure everyone has a great time.

Schedule

Saturday

9:30 - 10:00

Registration and coffee

Come along to Powershop, pick up your schwag bag, and have some delicious Apollo coffee.
10:00 - 10:30

Introduction

Meet the lovely people you'll be spending the weekend with and get an idea of the day ahead.
10:30 - 12:15

Installfest / Try Ruby

Install Rails on your computer - once you're done, try out programming with Ruby.
12:15 - 12:45

Lunch

Eat some tasty foods.
12:45 - 13:15

Talks

Hear from Amy Palamountain, Wellington's very own GitHubber, about version control and social coding.
13:15 - 13:45

The Robot Game

Instruct a human robot to get_cupcake.
13:45 - 16:30

Make an App

Go through the Rails Girls app guide to create your own Ruby on Rails app.
16:30 - 16:45

Wrap up

Finish up for the day and hear a bit about tomorrow.

Sunday

9:30 - 10:00

Coffee and mingling

Hang out with your new friends, drink some delicious Apollo coffee.
10:00 - 11:00

Welcome, lightning talks, and bentobox

Get ready for the day and hear talks from Philippa Dawe and Charlie Ablett. Then learn about how the webstack works using the analogy of delicious food.
11:00 - 12:00

How to protect yourself from your future self: TDD with team Abletech

If you've finished getting your app together, learn how to make sure that apps do what they're meant to do using tests.

OR
11:00 - 12:00

Continuing on with app guide

Continue making your very own Ruby on Rails application.
12:00 - 12:30

Programming and CSS Basics - optional

Hear from Maria Neise and Merrin Macleod about the very basic building blocks of programming and CSS.
12:30 - 13:00

Lunch

More tasty foods. Omnomnomnomnom.
13:00 - 16:00

Extension streams

Choose between learning to make a game in Ruby with Roger; learning to make your app look the way you want with Matt; and working with your coach to do the things you want to do with your app.
16:00 - 17:00

Wrap up, show off, sponsor talks

Check out what people have been working on; hear from representatives of our lovely gold sponsors; and hear from Nahum Wild about getting involved with the wider Ruby and Rails communities in Wellington, New Zealand, and internationally.
17:00 - 18:30

Party!

Have some beverages and finger food, meet sponsors, and hang out with your new friends. Let your hair down. Or don't. We believe your hair orientation is not super important to your ability to party.
